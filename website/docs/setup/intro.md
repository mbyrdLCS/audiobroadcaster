# Setup Guide

This guide is for whoever sets up Audio Broadcaster at your church — typically a minister, elder, or anyone comfortable installing software on a Mac.

## What It Does

Audio Broadcaster takes the audio from your church's live stream and makes it available to anyone on your church Wi-Fi through the **B1 app**. Congregation members open B1, tap the Audio section, and can hear the service louder on their phone — no app download, no account, no setup on their end.

**Translation is also built in** — listeners can optionally switch to a translated version in their language (Spanish, French, Korean, and more), making it useful for multilingual congregations.

## What You Need

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| A Mac | macOS 10.13 or newer |
| Audio input from your live stream | USB audio interface, mixer output, or built-in mic |
| Church Wi-Fi | The Mac and all phones must be on the same network |
| B1 app configured | The listener URL gets added as an external page in B1 |
| Internet | Only needed for translation — audio works offline |

## How It Works

1. You open Audio Broadcaster on the Mac — it starts automatically
2. The app shows a listener URL (like `http://192.168.1.5:3000/listen_v2.html`)
3. That URL is already set up in your B1 app — congregation members just tap it
4. They hear the service audio on their phone, with the option to switch languages
